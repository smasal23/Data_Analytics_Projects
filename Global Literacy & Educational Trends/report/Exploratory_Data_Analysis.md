# Exploratory Data Analysis

## Part 1 --- Univariate Analysis (Key Insights)

### 1. Adult Literacy Distribution

-   Strong negative skew; median more representative than mean.
-   Distribution is definitively non-normal → non-parametric tests
    required.
-   Global literacy is pulled down by a lower-tail minority of
    low-literacy countries.
-   Strategic focus should target bottom-tier countries for maximum
    global impact.

### 2. Youth vs Adult Literacy (Generational Signal)

-   Youth literacy exceeds adult literacy globally.
-   Both distributions non-normal; youth literacy more concentrated at
    high levels.
-   Indicates long-term educational investment success.
-   Adult literacy deficit will persist generationally → adult programs
    remain critical.

### 3. Literacy Stability Index

-   Most countries show stable literacy progress; small volatile lower
    tail exists.
-   Volatility often linked to conflict/political disruption.
-   Stability index should be used as an education investment risk
    metric.

### 4. GDP per Capita Distribution

-   Raw GDP extremely right-skewed; log transformation statistically
    required.
-   Median GDP better benchmark than mean for policy comparison.
-   Strong positive correlation between log GDP and literacy.
-   Wealth enables literacy, but policy determines conversion
    efficiency.

### 5. Gender Gap Distribution

-   Median near parity but extreme outliers exist.
-   Gender gap statistically confirmed.
-   Concentrated cluster of high-gap countries persists.
-   Targeted female education programs yield highest marginal returns.

### 6. YoY Growth Spikes

-   Extreme skew indicates unreliable single-year growth metrics.
-   Large spikes driven by survey/reporting changes.
-   YoY unsuitable as primary KPI.
-   Multi-year metrics required for policy evaluation.

### 7. 5-Year Literacy Momentum

-   Structurally positive and statistically significant.
-   Much more stable than YoY growth.
-   Confirms sustained global literacy improvement.
-   5-year window is the correct policy evaluation horizon.

## Part 2 --- Bivariate Analysis (Key Insights)

### 8. Literacy vs GDP (Ceiling Effect)

-   Strong positive correlation, but not deterministic.
-   Resource-rich underperformers and low-income overperformers both
    exist.
-   Governance and institutional quality mediate GDP-to-literacy
    conversion.

### 9. Education Development Index (EDI) vs Literacy

-   Near-perfect correlation.
-   Structural education development more predictive than GDP.
-   Development evaluation should weight EDI over income metrics.

### 10. Spending Efficiency vs Literacy

-   Weak direct relationship with literacy.
-   Efficiency alone does not drive outcomes.
-   Infrastructure and schooling duration stronger levers in low-income
    contexts.

### 11. Regional Gender Gap Structure

-   Africa highest gender gap; Asia moderate; Europe/Americas near
    parity.
-   Regional differences statistically significant.
-   Gender policy must be regionally differentiated.

### 12. Schooling Years vs Literacy Momentum

-   Negative correlation due to saturation effect.
-   High-schooling countries plateau; low-schooling countries catch up.
-   Education ROI highest in low-schooling regions.

## Part 3 --- Time Series Analysis (Key Insights)

### 13. Global Literacy Trend (1990--2023)

-   Steady upward global trajectory.
-   Youth literacy consistently above adult literacy.
-   Generational lag ensures adult deficit persists.
-   Adult literacy requires dedicated intervention.

### 14. Regional Convergence

-   Regional differences statistically significant.
-   Africa and South Asia on steep catch-up curve.
-   Developed regions plateauing.
-   Marginal returns highest in converging regions.

### 15. Top Improvers vs Stagnators

-   Structurally distinct categories confirmed statistically.
-   Catch-up economies dominate improvers.
-   Near-ceiling or governance-constrained countries stagnate.
-   Diagnostic intervention required for anomalous decliners.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Cross-Cutting Structural Insights

-   Structural education development explains literacy outcomes more
    than raw GDP.
-   Gender inequality is regionally concentrated and economically
    consequential.
-   Literacy growth follows a catch-up dynamic with saturation at high
    baseline levels.
-   Policy converts economic capacity into education outcomes; wealth
    alone is insufficient.
